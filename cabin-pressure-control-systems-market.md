<p><p>Cabin pressure control systems are essential for maintaining optimal pressure in aircraft cabins during flight. The market is categorized into two main types: fully automatic and semi-automatic systems. Fully automatic systems autonomously regulate cabin pressure, adjusting based on altitude and other parameters without pilot intervention, ensuring consistent comfort and safety. In contrast, semi-automatic systems require some degree of manual input from pilots to manage pressure levels, offering a balance between automation and control. Both types enhance passenger comfort and flight safety.</p></p>
