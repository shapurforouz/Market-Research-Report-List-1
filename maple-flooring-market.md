<p><p>The maple flooring market is primarily divided into two categories: soft maple and hard maple. Soft maple, derived from species like red maple, offers a more affordable option with a lighter shade and moderate durability, making it suitable for residential use. In contrast, hard maple, often sourced from sugar maple, is denser, more durable, and features a rich, attractive grain, making it ideal for high-traffic areas and commercial applications. Both types cater to varying aesthetic and functional needs within the flooring industry.</p></p>
