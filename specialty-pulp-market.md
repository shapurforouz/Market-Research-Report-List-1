<p><p>The specialty pulp market is primarily categorized into softwood pulp and hardwood pulp, each serving distinct applications. Softwood pulp, derived from coniferous trees, is known for its long fibers, providing strength and durability, making it ideal for products like high-quality paper and packaging. In contrast, hardwood pulp, sourced from broadleaf trees, has shorter fibers, offering smoothness and absorbency, which is preferred for fine paper, tissues, and specialty applications. These two types cater to varying industry demands, impacting production and pricing strategies.</p></p>
