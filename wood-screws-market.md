<p><p>The wood screws market is primarily segmented into three material types: carbon steel, stainless steel, and brass. Carbon steel screws are known for their strength and affordability, making them popular for general woodworking applications. Stainless steel screws offer corrosion resistance, ideal for outdoor or moisture-prone environments. Brass screws provide aesthetic appeal and are often used for decorative applications, though they are less durable than the other options. Each material serves specific needs within the woodworking industry, catering to functionality, durability, and visual preferences.</p></p>
